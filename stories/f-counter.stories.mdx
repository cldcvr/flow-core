## label

<f-divider></f-divider>

Label property defines the numeric count on the counter. For larger numeric values label truncates
according to the rules mentioned below.

<br />

<table class="custom-color-table">
	<tbody>
		<tr>
			<th>Number</th>
			<th>Rule</th>
			<th>Examples</th>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td>Less than 10</td>
			<td>zero gets appended before number.</td>
			<td>01, 05, 09</td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td>Between 10 and 999</td>
			<td>actual number gets displayed</td>
			<td>12, 467, 899</td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td>Between 999 and 10,000</td>
			<td>
				Use of K to annotate Thousand
				<br />
				Note: Round of the number upto one place of decimal.
			</td>
			<td>
				1732 -> 1.7K
				<br />
				8760 -> 8.8K
			</td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td>Between 10,000 and 1 million</td>
			<td>
				Annotate with K and round off the number to the nearest thousand.
				<br />
				Note: Do not use decimals
			</td>
			<td>
				34657 -> 35k
				<br />
				887650 -> 888K
			</td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td>Between 1 million and 10 million</td>
			<td>
				Use M to annotate Million
				<br />
				Note: Round of the number upto one place of decimal.
			</td>
			<td>
				1100000 -> 1.1M
				<br />
				2560000 -> 2.6M
			</td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<td>10 million and above</td>
			<td>
				Annotate with M and round off the number to the nearest thousand.
				<br />
				Note: Do not use decimals
			</td>
			<td>
				5500000 -> 55M
				<br />
				1280000 -> 13M
				<br /> 678000000 -> 678M
			</td>
		</tr>
	</tbody>
</table>
